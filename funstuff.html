# Podcasts I love podcasts! I discovered the magical world of podcasts sometime in 2017. Haven't stopped since. A sample of some great podcasts: - [The Anthropocene Reviewed](https://soundcloud.com/theanthropocenereviewed) by the incredible [John Green](https://www.johngreenbooks.com/):
This is my favorite podcast, by a country mile! - [Seen and the Unseen](https://seenunseen.in/) by [Amit Varma](https://amitvarma.com/):
India specific podcast, lots of social science and philosophy. Long form ([sometimes > 2hr long](https://twitter.com/amitvarma/status/1376048619548073988?s=20)) interview/discussion. - [Dear Hank and John](https://dear-hank-john-a5cf29b8.simplecast.com/) by [Hank](https://hankgreen.com/) and [John Green](https://www.johngreenbooks.com/):
where the two brothers answer your questions, give you dubious advice, and bring you the news from Mars and AFC Wimbledon. Seriously just try it! - [Left, Right and Centre](https://www.kcrw.com/news/shows/left-right-center):
In (former host) Josh Barro's words - "your civilized yet provocative antidote to the self-contained opinion bubbles that dominate political debates" - [Computing Up](https://computingup.com/) with [Michael Littman](https://www.littmania.com/) and [Dave Ackley](https://twitter.com/livcomp):
Conversations about computing writ large - [Capitalisn't](https://www.capitalisnt.com/) with [Luigi Zingales](https://faculty.chicagobooth.edu/luigi-zingales) and [Bethany McLean](https://twitter.com/bethanymac12):
economics podcast that talks about what's working in capitalism and what isn't - [Casual Inference](https://casualinfer.libsyn.com/) hosted by [Lucy Dâ€™Agostino McGowan](https://www.lucymcgowan.com/) and Ellie Murray:
podcast about statistics, causal inference, epidemiology, and a bunch of related things. - [The Gerrymandering Project](https://fivethirtyeight.com/tag/the-gerrymandering-project/) by the good folks at FiveThirtyEight and also [Dave Wasserman](https://twitter.com/redistrict):
podcast (plus interactives) about the fascinating complexities of gerrymandering and more importantly self-sorting! - [Rachman Review](https://www.ft.com/rachman-review) with [Gideon Rachman](https://twitter.com/gideonrachman):
A podcast about international politics by the Financial Times. - [Sway](https://www.nytimes.com/column/sway) with [Kara Swisher](https://twitter.com/karaswisher):
Interview podcast unpacking power and technology - [Volts](https://www.volts.wtf/) by [David Roberts](https://twitter.com/drvolts):
A newsletter/podcast about clean energy and politics. (Pretty wonky, which is why I love it!) # Books I read a lot, with occasional droughts. My Goodreads profile, which can be found [here](https://www.goodreads.com/user/show/16471733-ajinkya-kokandakar), is reasonably up to date.
